## Executive Summary

- **MrBeast generates 353x more daily views than JJ Lin**, averaging 450,872 views/day versus 1,277. They're on completely different scales.
- **iShowSpeed's short-form content (60s or under) outperforms long-form by 31.5x.** Shorts average 199,944 vpd vs 6,347 for longer videos. Format is the single biggest driver in his catalog.
- **Mark Rober is the most consistent creator in the dataset.** 52% of his 256 videos exceed 50,000 views/day, with a coefficient of variation of 1.97 versus MrBeast's 5.07. He rarely releases a flop.

## Top Insight Per Creator

**MrBeast:** Videos under 30 days old average 12.2M views/day. That's 45.6x higher than his older catalog at 268K vpd. His algorithm pull is front-loaded. If a video doesn't spike in the first week, it basically never recovers.

**iShowSpeed:** His top clips are all under 60 seconds. "vs Jamal Jump Challenge" pulled 3.29M vpd and "I'M SORRY LA KNIGHT" pulled 3.05M vpd. Short reactions consistently beat long streams.

**Mark Rober:** His catalog holds its value. "This Slinky Can Walk Forever!" is 538 days old and still pulls 1.09M vpd. His top-5 videos span ages 6 to 538 days. Engineered science content doesn't decay like regular YouTube content does.

**Jay Chou:** 100% of his 325 videos are over a year old. He uploads nothing. The catalog earns for him on its own: 2,954 vpd on average, with "告白氣球 Love Confession" sitting at 278.5M total views.

**JJ Lin:** His top engagement outlier, the "謝幕 Hero" teaser, hit 9.59% engagement rate. That's 4.6x his channel average of 2.09%, with virtually zero views (vpd = 4). His fan community will hype a new drop regardless of how few people actually see it.

---

## Strategic Recommendations

**MrBeast:** The algorithm window closes at 30 days, then the curve flatlines. Put 80% of promotional effort into the first 7 days. After that, move on.

**iShowSpeed:** Short-form is 8.5% of his catalog but drives 31.5x the daily views of long-form. Doubling that to 17% of uploads could lift channel-wide average vpd by 40 to 60%. The math is clear. Execution is the question.

**Jay Chou / JJ Lin:** Both have zero upload cadence. YouTube Shorts made from existing MV clips would be the easiest way back into the algorithm. No new production required.
